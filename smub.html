SMUB - Smart Modular Urban Bog

2020

This is a 128-day academic project to turn a place in the city centre called Hobujaama from a thoughtless place to a more meaningful place where people can spend great time and get empowered emotionally.

RESEARCH


We conducted research in order to understand and empathise with the users around the Hobujaama and then define pain and intervention points to turn the Hobujaama area into a peaceful place.

We also used several kinds of maps from user group, stakeholder, journey, macro trends, to giga map so as to understand and visualize the players, pain points, intervention points with values, measures, and trends and their relations and connections.

IDEATION


Since Hobujaams lack color and nature, we found Green Technology more interesting and came up with combining Bog and technology for addressing human’s 5 senses. We coined the term “Smart Modular Urban Bog (SMUB)” for our concept while visualizing basic elements of SMUB one by one.

Our inspiration came from Bog which is a type of wetland ecosystem made by spongy peaty soil.

PROTOTYPE & TEST


We started from creating a landscape plan so as to describe SMUB from bird’s view and end with a minimal modular allocation plan. After that, we defined how SMUB really works, what happens, what people could do there, how it changes people’s behaviour.

Users can get a relaxation effect through their five senses, for instance, people can get coolness and warmness around the natural air conditioning on the pine-tree benches. This relaxation effect is intended to change the meaning of Hobujaama from a voiceless to peaceful place.

CONCLUSION


With SMUB, the new meanings are added to current meanings and the priority could be changed. People can not only get a relaxation through several sensations all together but also have an engagement on the place through an inspirational and educational environment.

And Hobujaama would have a potential to become a leading model which balances citizens, community, and environment.
